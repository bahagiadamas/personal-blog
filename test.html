On May 10th and 12th, 2023, I had the opportunity to be part of an initiative aimed at empowering small-scale shrimp farmers in Kebumen. Through this event, we introduced the Jagoan Tambak program, designed to help farmers optimize their water quality management and improve their shrimp cultivation efficiency.


Water quality management is one of the most crucial aspects of aquaculture. During the workshop, my team and I shared practical and data-driven techniques, helping farmers understand how factors such as pH levels, oxygen concentration, and water temperature impact shrimp growth.


Beyond the educational sessions, we also introduced the JALA App, a digital platform that enables farmers to monitor their water quality and track their farm’s progress in real-time. This application allows them to make more informed decisions based on accurate data, helping them manage their farms more efficiently and sustainably.


Reflections from Kebumen

Seeing the enthusiasm of shrimp farmers as they embraced new knowledge and applied it to their practices was an invaluable experience for me. Programs like these demonstrate that with education and technology, we can help small-scale farmers grow and navigate industry challenges with confidence.
